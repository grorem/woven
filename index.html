MFA Class of Spring 2015
National Retail Federation
The Challenge

It soon became clear that this was not going to be a simple redesign of a website, but so much more a tool to be used by an array of different parts of a large organization tasked with representing one of the largest industries in the United States; retail.

With this project I wore more hats than just design. My hand touched the information architecture, content strategy, of course design, and taking over for a project manager in charge of all the existing content as well as new content, around a decades worth, making it to the new site.

The Process

I traveled to Washington D.C. with a few collegues to conduct stakeholder interviews with the client which kicked off our involvement with the project they needed accomplished.

Content and Design Space

Shortly thereafter, my digital trail of contribution started by undertaking an enormous content-audit which was more than just a long slog through a spreadsheet. It was vital in the effort to categorize and keep track of much existing content, as well as inform the new information architecture and design templates of a website which reached far beyond simple end-user content.


Interaction Design

Working closely with the team, I created these wireframes in our effort to come up with the templates that so much content and funtionality would be well fit. Starting with hand-drawn sketches of ideas for quick and light iteration, I then moved the ideas to this highly-annotated form to lead discussion with the client regarding our recommendation for how to present different content and functionality in different templates with one main breakpoint for responsive display.




Content Management

Over the last 2 months of the project, I took over for one of the project managers who had been in charge of all the new site's content on our end. However, a situation arose and I had to step in and do about 4 months worth of work, which was neglected, within the remaining 2 months. This included making extensive use of the content management system we had set up, keeping track of old content that was to be imported into the new site, working with Quality Assurance to make sure content being imported was up to standards, as well as heading ad-hoc teams to accomplish this importation of content. This also included flying back to the client's offices in Washington D.C. for a week, working with their teams and our development team, and training their team on the CMS, so future involvement from me going forward with new content would not be necessary.

The Win

More than just a new skin on a website was made. A more engaging and usable experience for the end user and a more usefull collaboration platform for internal teams found positive impact on everything from analytics on news published on the site to changing some of the internal collaboration flows of the organization for the better.

We came in as the smallest class, but instantly became fast friends. Representing every timezone in the United States, but still united with that cat meow.

We now present our woven tapestry of work that has occupied the last two years of our lives. Together every 6 months, and always on each others minds. We are sad to be at the end of this journey, but have made friends that will last the rest of our lives.

Kelly

I found my joy! Happiness in a pink heart!

I started my MFA off with the goal of regaining my Joy for design. At this point, my studies have included: Experimentation with paint and splatter to reconnect with myself as a maker; Typographic one-a-days to help overcome my fear of failure; Speculative public installations that allow me to dream big; Participatory projects such as chalk circles,

hearts and community gardens that I did without understanding how they were relevant to my study until afterwards when I realized that they each brought together the elements of exactly the topics I was studying; and in-depth research on the topics of joy and happiness, our culture’s work ethic, faith and the feminine role.

What I am trying to figure out now is how to articulate how Joy relates to our culture’s ‘work hard’ work ethic, the feminine role, faith and how the work I am doing ties it all together.

Ethan

Symbols And Myths...my flag story.

The design of the US National Flag is a story of mythic proportions. Unfortunately, that story is based more on myth than fact. Some might even be so inclined to suggest that the design of the flag may have occurred through some form of divine intervention. And, as is often the case, there are those who are willing to believe what they wish to be true. If given the choice, most would probably just prefer that the myth of the design of the flag continue to be

taught in perpetuity, with blatant disregard for historical facts. From a design perspective, the flag is an amazing design artifact. It is a rudimentary design with basic, simple colors. Unfortunately, most flags are designed this way … they are very basic. They are generally designed with a large area of color, for instance, in the canton area. Flags usually include a geometric shape or a symbol.

My original flag designs were rooted in protest, attempting to communicate through visual messaging and graffiti.

Hybrid Self

Changes within myself to better adapt to my life in the United States

Holidays are great time to create happy memories and to be with your loved ones. It is also a time when everyone slows themselves down, takes break from their daily routines, and reflects on all their blessings. Before I decorated Christmas trees with my husband year after year or sang Christmas carols or wrapped Christmas presents for kids in the family, I grew up celebrating a very different holiday that was as big as Christmas. The excitement and preparation for that special day was just as grand.

Growing up in Pakistan, I celebrated Eid (or Eid-ul-fitr), which, in my opinion, is the biggest holiday in the country. Eid-ul-Fitr is one of two Eid Holidays that fall in one year. Eid-al-Fitr is the first day of the Islamic month know as Shawwal. It marks the end of Ramadan, which is a month of dawn to sunset fasting and prayer. This holiday lasts three days. Eid-al-Fitr is a time to be happy, to give to less fortunate, and to show unity. During this holiday, many people give good charity called Zakat, such as barley, dates, flour or money to the poor. It is also a time to forgive and forget.

This thesis is an exploration of hybrid cultural identity through visual design. My process, in which personal memories are recontextualized into new visual form, is a demonstration of reflective self-discovery and cultural connection through making. Through this work. I developed my voice as a designer and achieved a deeper understanding of my dual, and at times conflicting, cultural heritage.

Dual Ties

Personal Practice Informs Inspired Teaching

Through years of experience, I have become aware of a number of dualities that comprise who I am as a person, graphic designer, artist and instructor. My work has focused on ways to develop the interaction between these dualities to create a more complete and whole person.

At one time, I viewed my personal practice and teaching as opposing dualities. My personal practice seemed to be part of my creative life and teaching part of my professional life. Just like my artist and designer dualities, personal practice and teaching would fight for my time, voice and attention.

The development of my personal practice allowed me to gradually gained respect for all my dualities and how they can work together to create a unified teaching philosophy and methodology.

Through the exploration of dualities and discoveries of their dual ties, I created The Graphic Designer’s Awareness workbook for senior portfolio students at Stephens College. The workbook is intended to help graphic design students, teachers, and practitioners develop personal awareness while building design confidence. Once a student has completed the workbook, they will have a greater working knowledge of how to sustain their creative flame for design and inspire others throughout their careers.

Through personal exploration, I have bridged the gap between my artist and designer and other opposing dualities. I understand how the unifying influences of these dual ties have made me a complete being. My personal practice now strongly informs, inspires and re-ignites my love for teaching graphic design.

Through personal exploration, I have bridged the gap between these once opposing forces, and their dual ties have now made me a complete being. My personal practice now strongly informs, inspires and re-ignites my love for teaching graphic design. My discoveries led me to the reali-

zation that personal creative practice and teaching are not separate entities. But rather, underlying foundations that seamlessly support each other as they co-exist. I further realized the development of personal creative practice and related beliefs can inform inspired teaching and practice.

Daniel

Wellspring – My Typeagogy Journey

My business ethic has been influenced by mentors and others who have helped me form my management and design style. Now at the later half of my career, I see this opportunity to take my years of business experience and put it to work in education as a teacher and mentor to motivate my students to develop/express their creativity in Graphic Design.

Self-discovery of my formal and informal education has led me to understand that I have been creating type since elementary school when I experimented with hand drawn type brochures before I even understood what typography or Graphic Design was in the context of the world. All I knew was that I loved to create designs.

A wellspring is defined as a source of water, a spring or a stream. This can be related to finding the source of creativity that each person has inside.

Another example of native type I found on the island of Cozumel, Mexico was the word “El Moro”. It was tucked away on a side street back from the water line of the main town or maybe the only town on Cozumel. This alleyway lead to some deserted homes and finally to the end of the houses in this area. We were looking for a pharmacy to pick up some band-aids, but were having difficulty due to the fact that we didn’t speak much Spanish.

Searches on the Internet revealed that “El Moro” refers to the Moors of Spain. When I later consulted Lorena Sheridan-Howard about the origin of “El Moro”, she said it was now a common name, but it did have origins in the Moorish tradition in Spain. The lettering extended into a broken plaster free area, so I had to imagine what all of the letters looked like. I had only “M”, “O” & “R” to work with, but the overall typeface seemed to reflect the feel of the typeface, Fritz Quadrata, with a swirl of Old Mexico.

Wendy

I said simply, “I want to make beautiful things.”

The most basic thing I knew when I arrived at VCFA was that I wanted to work with my hands. I am interested in design as a body-mind-heart experience. When I limit myself to the computer screen, I have limited my hands and as a result, my heart too. I have severely curtailed my physical connection to the work when all the variables are on screen. With the computer, I can easily correct mistakes, it’s hard to make a mess, I am limited physically to my right hand attached to the mouse (or stylus), and my range of motion is tiny. I don’t integrate any other parts of my body with that work. I also don’t integrate my heart as I touch the same mouse and the same keyboard regardless

of what I am making on screen. There are no textures, no smells, no unique sounds and instead only two dimensional vision when I work on the computer. For me, technology is a soul-killer if it’s the dominant tool in my work. Based on research about writing by hand (as opposed to typing): Daniel M. Oppenheime, a psychologist from UCLA, states ‘Maybe it (handwriting) helps you think better. I don’t think it’s a huge leap to suggest that working with your hands in whatever you are doing helps you to integrate and process whatever you are working on at a deeper and more integrated level.’”

Each color and its level of saturation reflects a particular emotion and its intensity. Working with color is my favorite aspect of these watermarks. Each time I pull a sheet from its container I get a rush from the sheer brilliance of whatever hue is present in its multitude of variations.
